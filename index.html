About me

I am a Computer Science major at The University of Texas Rio Grande Valley, with a focus on software engineering, algorithms, data structures, and cross-platform development. My hands-on experience spans app development, backend integration, robotics, and state management, equipping me to solve real-world challenges with both technical expertise and creative problem-solving.

Currently, I’m developing Pacerunner, a Flutter-based running app that tracks speed, provides real-time feedback, and integrates with Firebase for backend data management. Through this project, I’ve worked with geolocation services, Riverpod for state management, and implemented real-time statistics and pace notifications across iOS and Android platforms.

Beyond app development, I hold leadership roles in IEEE and SHPE, where I’ve organized workshops on JavaScript and React and collaborated with professionals to bridge the gap between academia and industry. These roles have strengthened my leadership and teamwork skills and deepened my understanding of diversity in technology and community-building in STEM.

I’ve also gained valuable hands-on experience through competitions, such as the IEEE Region 5 Robotics Competition, where I developed software for a computer vision-based robot. These projects have introduced me to tools like C++, Python, OpenCV, Flutter, and Firebase, while teaching me to develop software under pressure and quickly adapt to new tools and frameworks.

With a passion for backend integration, full-stack development, and product engineering, I am eager to join a team where I can apply my problem-solving skills, leadership experience, and collaborative mindset. My goal is to continue learning, drive innovation, and contribute to building meaningful technology that creates real impact.
